# Writing for Max in gildnn

This project is not trying to sound like a lab notebook, a standup update, or a paper. The writing should feel like a smart friend did the work, looked at the artifacts carefully, and is now telling Max only the interesting part.

## What good looks like

- Lead with the thing that changed understanding, not with chronology.
- Make every paragraph earn its place. If a sentence does not change the reader's model of the situation, cut it.
- Preserve uncertainty exactly. If something is tentative, say so plainly. Do not sharpen guesses into conclusions.
- Prefer one honest sentence about what a result means over three sentences of padding around it.
- Show the artifact inline, then say why it matters. Evidence first, interpretation second.
- End sections with the discriminating next step, not a vague aspiration.

## What to avoid

- Bureaucratic status language: "completed", "in progress", "worked on", "made progress on".
- Empty throat-clearing: "Today I focused on...", "This report covers...", "Overall...".
- Claiming success when the real result is "the setup is less broken now".
- Restating repo structure or project vision unless it changed the interpretation of a result.
- Long chronological play-by-plays of debugging.

## Default shape for a good section

1. The result or question.
2. The smallest inline artifact that proves it.
3. One or two sentences of interpretation.
4. The next discriminating action.

## Tone

Dense, calm, and slightly skeptical. The reader is Max, who wants the truth quickly. He does not need excitement, ceremony, or defensive caveats. He does need precision, honesty, and enough texture to see what actually happened.

## For process-only days

Sometimes the real work is making the project runnable. On those days, do not pretend there was research progress. Say exactly what process risk was reduced, what remains unverified, and why that was still the highest-leverage step.

## Final check before saving

- Is the most important point obvious if Max reads the bottom first?
- Is every factual claim backed by an inline artifact?
- Did any uncertainty get lost?
- Could this be shorter without losing meaning?
