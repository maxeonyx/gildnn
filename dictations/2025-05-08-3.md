## Stretch goal: dynamic computation / dynamic depth

"What I wanted to see, actually, in this guildinian, this ultra-flexible recurrent transformer, is I wanted to see dynamic computation and dynamic hierarchical encodings. I believe that that's in the original vision.md. Can you confirm that? That is a completely separate research direction, but I would love to see it be integrated somehow."

"Basically, what that means is that you use the same model weights multiple times in sequence to produce a single output sequence token. You iterate on the same internal states, and you have some kind of self-loss prediction that tells you when to terminate that loop at inference time. In training, you run multiple rollouts of the output to get a better idea. You have to compute the output with one iteration. You compute it with two iterations. You compute it with three iterations. You compute it with four iterations, etc. Maybe like an exponential schedule of those. I don't know. And then you record the loss. Is it getting better or worse? And then the loss predictor output head also gets trained to predict the loss of the different levels of depth, so that you then at inference time can have an estimate over what is the best amount of iterations for the next output token."

"That's a whole other research direction that I was interested in seeing originally, and I would actually love to see in here as well. And I believe there's plenty of research on this out there. And again, I'm not interested necessarily in seeing this research direction combined with my cortical network stuff to start with. I'm interested in seeing, let's say, an ordinary recurrent model or a transformer that is used in this fashion, and then finally at the end, if these things pan out, combining them together. Very small building blocks, concrete write-ups with clear outcomes. If it's not clear, keep working. If you can't have everything referenced to a particular rollout, run more experiments."
